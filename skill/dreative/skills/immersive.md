# Dreative Specialist Skill — Immersive Worlds & Spatial Transitions

Selected immersive treatment owns continuity, presence or environmental progression across compositions and proves the handoff temporally.

## Contract

Follow `../references/SKILL_CONTRACT.md`. Dependencies: `motion`, `interaction`,
`media`, `ux`, `mobile`; add `3d` only when real spatial rendering is used.
Deliver a named persistent-stage architecture, travel map, route/scroll logic,
budgets, accessible fallback, and runtime evidence.

Load when the page should behave as a place or journey rather than a sequence of
independent sections.

## 0. Explore before recipes

Record three original brand-native world concepts before opening
`../recipes/immersive-recipes.md`. Define the world's invariant, what persists,
how the visitor travels, and why that behavior belongs to this subject.

## 1. Hard gates

- Content, routes, navigation, keyboard, forms, and direct URLs work without the
  persistent stage (`ux.functional`, `content.visible`).
- Every traveling object has a per-section berth/state; no text/control
  occlusion or pointer interception (`spatial.noOcclusion`).
- Mobile, reduced motion, loading, low power, and runtime failure receive a
  purpose-designed alternative (`mobile.required`, `motion.reducedMotion`,
  `effects.runtimeFallback`).
- Preloaders report real asset progress; sound is opt-in and nonessential.
- Heavy scenes pass asset-weight and frame-time budgets with proof.

## 2. World architecture

Use one persistent root stage when continuity is the concept. Routes/chapters
publish scene state—palette, camera, active objects, fog, media—rather than
mounting unrelated canvases. Something meaningful persists through transitions:
subject, camera, material, type, or color logic.

Scroll-as-travel maps progress to semantic chapters. Avoid mandatory snap on
long content; preserve native reading and direct navigation. Text remains DOM.

## 3. Creative structure

At expressive/award choose diversity or development. Immersive concepts often
benefit from development: one persistent object/system changes roles across
chapters while quieter typography/material/input responses support it. Do not add
unrelated scenes merely to satisfy a mechanism count.

Spatial signatures follow `award.spatialSignature`; organic subjects and generic
orbs remain constrained by the 3D rules.

## 4. Planning and verification

Plan the stage owner, chapter states, transition persistence, travel map, loading,
direct-entry behavior, input equivalents, mobile/reduced-motion translation,
performance budget, and fallback.

Verify cold URLs, route forward/back, full scroll and reverse, focus/keyboard,
mobile, reduced motion, occlusion at multiple progress points, real asset load,
console, frame time, transferred weight, and failure fallback.

The persistent stage and every chapter transition map to typed motion moments;
record scene/camera/application state at controlled progress, not screenshots or
console status alone.

Recipe reference: `../recipes/immersive-recipes.md`, after exploration only.
Canonical v9 requires one continuity owner and multi-section persistence. A
hero-only canvas followed by unrelated static sections fails. Calm rests must
inherit, echo or prepare environmental state.

The continuity owner is implementation architecture, not a prose label. Record
the shared state/render owner and typed handoff target for every chapter,
including the persisted object/state and receiving implementation. Repeating a
decorative line or color without shared runtime state is not continuity.
